Facebook Image Dimensions 2026

⏱ 5 {{ t.guide_min_read }} 📅 {{ t.guide_updated }}

Facebook is accessed on devices ranging from small phones to large desktop monitors, and its image display dimensions vary accordingly. Using precise dimensions ensures your images look sharp, are not cropped unexpectedly, and survive Facebook's compression with acceptable quality.

Facebook cover photo

SurfaceDimensionsNotes
Page cover photo820 × 312 px (desktop) / 640 × 360 px (mobile)Use 820×462 for both
Profile cover photo820 × 312 pxSafe area differs on mobile
Group cover photo1640 × 856 pxDisplayed at 820×428 on desktop

For page and profile cover photos, Facebook crops differently on desktop and mobile. A safe approach: upload at 820 × 462 px and keep key content in the central 820×312 area. The top and bottom 75 px may be cut on desktop.

Facebook feed post images

For link posts (when you share a URL), Facebook pulls the Open Graph image. Set your OG image to exactly 1200×630 px for consistent display. The minimum for link previews is 600×315 px, but 1200×630 is displayed at 2x on Retina screens.

Facebook Stories are displayed full-screen at 9:16. The top and bottom approximately 250 px are overlaid by the profile bar and action buttons, keep important content in the safe zone.

Facebook compression tips

  • Upload JPEG at quality 80–90 before posting, Facebook re-compresses regardless
  • Use PNG for graphics with text, Facebook's JPEG compression blurs text in graphics
  • Use sRGB colour space to avoid colour shifts
  • Avoid uploading images larger than the recommended dimensions, Facebook will scale down and apply additional compression
Key takeaway: The most important Facebook image size to get right is the cover photo (820×462 with a safe area of 820×312) and link post OG image (1200×630). For feed posts, 1080×1080 (square) or 1200×630 (landscape) are the safest choices. Always upload JPEG at 85+ quality in sRGB.
